Meteorology Command

B1+ Climate Tracker Interface

Central Database: Lexicon

Review advanced meteorological terminology and B1+ structures prior to deployment.

Blizzard
A severe snowstorm with high winds and low visibility.
If we hadn't checked the forecast, we would have been caught in the blizzard.
Drought
A prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall.
The prolonged drought has severely affected local agriculture.
Scorching
Very hot; boiling.
It's absolutely scorching outside; we must have the AC on.
Overcast
Covered with clouds; dull and grey.
The sky was overcast, suggesting that a storm was imminent.
Drizzle
Light rain falling in very fine drops.
It's not raining heavily, it's just a light drizzle.
Gale
A very strong wind.
The gale-force winds caused significant damage to the harbor.
Downpour
A sudden, heavy fall of rain.
We were completely soaked after being caught in the sudden downpour.
Humid
Marked by a relatively high level of water vapour.
The air feels incredibly humid and sticky today.
Sleet
Rain containing some ice, as when snow melts.
The rain quickly turned into sleet as the temperature dropped.
Mild
Not severe, cold, or harsh.
Winter in this coastal region is surprisingly mild.
Frostbite
Injury to body tissues caused by extreme cold.
Mountaineers must wrap up warmly to prevent frostbite.
Blow over
Phrasal Verb: When a storm loses its strength and ends.
Let's wait inside until the storm blows over.
