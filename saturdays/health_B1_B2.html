👩‍⚕️ HEALTH VOCABULARY - FRESHMEN 👨‍⚕️

Fit as a fiddle 🔊
/fɪt əz ə ˈfɪd.əl/
💪
To be in perfect health and physical condition.
Example: She exercises daily and is fit as a fiddle.
Under the weather 🔊
/ˈʌn.də ðə ˈweð.ər/
🤒
Feeling slightly ill or not in good spirits.
Example: He stayed home today because he felt a bit under the weather.
A clean bill of health 🔊
/ə kliːn bɪl əv helθ/
👨‍⚕️
A doctor's official statement that someone is healthy.
Example: The physical checkup gave her a clean bill of health.
Prescription 🔊
/prɪˈskrɪp.ʃən/
📝
A piece of paper with medical details for medicine.
Example: The nurse gave him a prescription for eye drops.
Wound 🔊
/wuːnd/
🩹
An injury, especially one with damage to the skin.
Example: The hero bandaged his sword wound quickly.
Pass Out 🔊
/pɑːs aʊt/
😵
To become unconscious for a short time (faint).
Example: The intense heat made the character pass out during training.
On the mend 🔊
/ɒn ðə mend/
🛌
Getting better after an illness or injury.
Example: His broken leg is finally on the mend.
Black and blue 🔊
/blæk ənd bluː/
🤕
Covered in bruises from being hit or falling.
Example: After the robot battle, his arm was black and blue.
Come down with 🔊
/kʌm daʊn wɪð/
🤧
To start to suffer from an illness.
Example: I think I'm coming down with the flu.
Swollen 🔊
/ˈswəʊ.lən/
🎈
Larger than usual due to an injury.
Example: After twisting his ankle, it became very swollen.
Check-up 🔊
/ˈtʃek.ʌp/
🩺
A general medical examination.
Example: I go to the dentist for a check-up every year.
Dizzy 🔊
/ˈdɪz.i/
💫
Feeling like everything is turning around.
Example: Standing up too quickly made her feel dizzy.
Surgery 🔊
/ˈsɜː.dʒər.i/
🏥
Medical treatment where a doctor cuts your body open.
Example: He needs to have surgery to repair his broken knee.
Symptom 🔊
/ˈsɪmp.təm/
⚠️
A physical feeling or problem that shows you are sick.
Example: A bad cough is a common symptom of a cold.
Infection 🔊
/ɪnˈfek.ʃən/
🦠
A disease in your body caused by bacteria or a virus.
Example: You must clean the cut to prevent a serious infection.
Throw up 🔊
/θrəʊ ʌp/
🤢
To vomit or empty the contents of your stomach.
Example: Eating the old food made him throw up in the bathroom.
