🐆 Level 4: The Amur Leopard

Read the passage carefully and answer True or False. (5 questions)

The Amur leopard, known for its thick, beautiful spotted coat, is one of the most critically endangered big cats on the planet. Native to the harsh, freezing climates of the Russian Far East and parts of northern China, this incredible apex predator has adapted to survive in extreme conditions. During the winter, their fur grows up to 7 centimeters long to protect them from the biting cold. However, despite their physical resilience, they are highly vulnerable to human activities.

The primary threats pushing the Amur leopard to the verge of extinction are relentless poaching and widespread habitat destruction. Poachers illegally hunt these leopards for their magnificent fur, which is sold on the black market for high prices. Additionally, illegal logging and agricultural expansion are rapidly wiping out the dense forests they call home, leaving them with less territory to hunt and breed. Furthermore, their main prey, such as roe deer and sika deer, are also being hunted by humans, leading to a severe food shortage for the leopards.

Fortunately, extensive conservation efforts are currently underway. Wildlife organizations have established safe sanctuaries and specialized anti-poaching units to patrol the forests. Captive breeding programs in zoos around the world are also working to maintain a healthy genetic pool. Although their population in the wild has slightly increased to around 120 individuals, they are still dangerously close to dying out completely.
